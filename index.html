Abstract

Aerial-Ground Person Re-identification (AGPReID) aims to match individuals across drastically different viewpoints, posing challenges due to significant variations in appearance, scale, and background context. Existing visual-based methods struggle to bridge the cross-view domain gap and often overlook semantic cues that could facilitate alignment. To address these limitations, we propose a novel method named MVP, which maps the view bias with prompts to enhance the alignment of cross-view representations.

MVP employs a two-stage training strategy to exploit the complementary strengths of vision-language alignment and aerial-ground semantics. In the first stage, view-aware textual prompts are used to align image-text pairs and inject perspective semantics into visual features. To further reinforce this alignment, we introduce the Aerial-Ground Similarity Consistency (AGSC) loss, which enables fine-grained consistency between cross-view features and their corresponding textual descriptions, facilitating robust adaptation across modalities.

In the second stage, neutral prompts guide the model to discard viewpoint-specific cues and refine identity representations. Such a progressive design enables MVP to first adapt to viewpoint bias and subsequently suppress it, resulting in more discriminative and view-invariant identity features.

Extensive experiments on the CARGO and AG-ReID datasets demonstrate that MVP achieves 70.2% Rank-1 and 65.7% mAP on CARGO, and 85.1% Rank-1 and 77.9% mAP on AG-ReID, outperforming previous methods and confirming the effectiveness of the proposed framework. The code was available at [your link].

Method

The figure shows our proposed MVP framework, which consists of two stages: bias transfer and unbiased guidance. In the first stage, inter-modal feature alignment is achieved by AGSC loss and alignment loss to map the viewpoint offsets between modes; in the second stage, unbiased bootstrapping is used to ensure feature consistency and solve the viewpoint bias problem
